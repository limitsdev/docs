## What is Limits?

Limits is an enterprise-grade policy management platform that enables you to create, manage, and enforce business rules and policies through an intuitive interface. The platform combines AI-powered policy generation with powerful configuration tools, allowing you to:

- **Create policies using natural language** - Describe your requirements in plain English, and AI will generate the policy configuration
- **Manage complex business rules** - Define conditions, limits, time windows, and velocity checks
- **Automate decision-making** - Automatically allow, block, or escalate requests based on your policies
- **Monitor and audit** - Track all policy evaluations through comprehensive logging
- **Integrate with your workflow** - Connect with Slack and other services for notifications

## Account Setup and Authentication

### Signing Up

1. Navigate to the sign-up page
2. Enter your email address and create a password
3. Verify your email address
4. Complete your profile information
5. You'll be redirected to the dashboard upon successful registration

### Signing In

1. Navigate to the sign-in page
2. Enter your email and password
3. Click "Sign In"
4. You'll be redirected to the dashboard

<Info>
The platform uses Clerk for authentication, providing secure and reliable user management.
</Info>

## Navigation Overview

The platform features a consistent sidebar navigation with the following main sections:

- **Dashboard** - Overview of your platform metrics and statistics
- **Policies** - Manage all your policies
- **Integrations** - Configure third-party integrations
- **Logs** - View and filter policy evaluation logs
- **Approvals** - Manage human approval requests

The sidebar is collapsible and can be minimized to show only icons for more screen space.

## First Steps Guide

### 1. Explore the Dashboard

- Review your current statistics (active policies, pending approvals, etc.)
- Familiarize yourself with the charts and visualizations

### 2. Create Your First Policy

- Navigate to the Assistant page
- Try creating a simple policy using natural language
- Example: "Block all requests over $10,000"

### 3. Review the Logs

- Check the Logs page to see how policies are being evaluated
- Use filters to find specific requests

### 4. Set Up Integrations

- Connect Slack for notifications
- Configure webhooks if needed

### 5. Generate API Keys

- Create API keys for programmatic access to the platform
